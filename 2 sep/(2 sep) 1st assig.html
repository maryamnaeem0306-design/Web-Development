Top Books of 2024 – Global Highlights

1. James by Percival Everett

Unanimously acclaimed, James emerged as the most recommended book across numerous year-end lists. It won both the National Book Award and the Kirkus Award and was a Booker Prize finalist Archyde Literary Hub Digg . This reimagining of Huckleberry Finn through Jim's eyes resonated deeply for its humanity and fresh perspective Voyaged Magazine Digg .

2. Martyr! by Kaveh Akbar

Akbar’s debut novel soared with critical praise and literary honors. Celebrated for its poetic prose and compelling protagonist, it became a NYT bestseller and received multiple shortlist nominations including for the National Book Award Wikipedia .

3. Creation Lake by Rachel Kushner

Shortlisted for the Booker Prize and praised for its thought-provoking espionage narrative, Creation Lake earned starred reviews from Kirkus and Publishers Weekly, as well as strong commentary from The Guardian and The New York Times Wikipedia Five Books .

4. The Book of Love by Kelly Link

This novel secured spots on Time’s "100 Must-Reads of 2024" and The New York Times’ "100 Notable Books." Critics hailed its vivid characters, immersive small-town setting, and enchanting blend of magic and mythology Wikipedia .

5. The Ministry of Time by Kaliane Bradley

This genre-blending debut weaves colonialism, identity, climate change, and romance. Digg ranked it top of their year-end list, praising its thematic depth and emotional resonance Digg Everand .

6. Funny Story by Emily Henry

A joyful, sparkling novel by bestselling author Emily Henry, recognized as a must-read by Time, Barnes & Noble, and Amazon editors alike PenguinRandomhouse.com .

7. Everyone Who Is Gone Is Here by Jonathan Blitzer

This powerful non-fiction work, detailing the humanitarian crisis at the U.S.–Mexico border, earned a spot among The New York Times Book Review's “10 Best Books of 2024” PenguinRandomhouse.com De Lou Africa .

8. Memory Piece by Lisa Ko

An inventive, emotionally rich novel spanning decades—from the 1980s to a visionary future—named one of Time’s must-reads of 2024 PenguinRandomhouse.com De Lou Africa .
